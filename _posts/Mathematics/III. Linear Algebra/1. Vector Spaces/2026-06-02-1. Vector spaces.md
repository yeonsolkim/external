**Definition 1.1.1.** Let $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the two-sorted first-order language with sorts $\mathcal F$ and $\mathcal V$. The sort $\mathcal F$ is intended to represent the field of scalars, and the sort $\mathcal V$ is intended to represent the set of vectors. The language contains constant symbols $0_F, 1_F$ of sort $\mathcal F$, function symbols $+_F, \cdot_F: F \times F \to F$, a unary function symbol $-_F: F \to F$, a constant symbol $0_V$ of sort $\mathcal V$, a function symbol $+_V: V \times V \to V$, a unary function symbol $-_V: V \to V$, and a function symbol$\mu: F \times V \to V$ for scalar multiplication.<br>
&emsp; An $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-structure $\mathcal {\mathcal M}$ consists of two underlying sets $F^{\mathcal M}$ and $V^{\mathcal M}$, together with interpretations of the symbols of $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$:

$$ \begin{aligned}0_F^{\mathcal M}, 1_F^{\mathcal M} \in F^{\mathcal M}, \qquad +_F^{\mathcal M}, \cdot_F^{\mathcal M}: F^{\mathcal M} \times F^{\mathcal M} \to F^{\mathcal M}, \qquad -_F^{\mathcal M}: F^{\mathcal M} \to F^{\mathcal M},\\
0_V^{\mathcal M} \in V^{\mathcal M}, \qquad +_V^{\mathcal M}: V^{\mathcal M} \times V^{\mathcal M} \to V^{\mathcal M}, \qquad -_V^{\mathcal M}: V^{\mathcal M} \to V^{\mathcal M},\end{aligned}$$

and

$$\mu^{\mathcal M}: F^{\mathcal M} \times V^{\mathcal M} \to V^{\mathcal M}.$$

&emsp; Let $T_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-theory consisting of the following axioms. First, the $\mathcal F$-sort is a field under $0_F, 1_F, +_F, -_F, \cdot_F$. Second, the $\mathcal V$-sort is an abelian group under $0_V, +_V, -_V$. That is, the following axioms hold.

1. $\forall u, v \in V \quad u +_V v = v +_V u.$
2. $\forall u, v, w \in V \quad (u +_V v) +_V w = u +_V (v +_V w).$
3. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V 0_V = v.$
4. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V (-_V v) = 0_V.$

Finally, the scalar multiplication symbol $\mu$ satisfies the following axioms:

5. $\forall v \in V \quad \mu(1_F, v) = v.$
6. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad \mu(a \cdot_F b, v) = \mu(a, \mu(b, v)),$
7. $\forall a \in F \ \forall u, v \in V \quad \mu(a, u +_V v) = \mu(a, u) +_V \mu(a, v),$
8. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad \mu(a +_F b, v) = \mu(a, v) +_V \mu(b, v),$
{:start="5"}

A model of $T_{\mathrm{vs}}$ is called a *vector space.*
