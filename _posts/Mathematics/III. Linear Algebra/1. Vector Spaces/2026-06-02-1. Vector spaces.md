**Definition 1.1.1.** Let $L_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the two-sorted first-order language with sorts $F$ and $V$. The sort $F$ is intended to represent the field of scalars, and the sort $V$ is intended to represent the set of vectors. The language contains constant symbols $0_F, 1_F$ of sort $F$, function symbols $+_F, \cdot_F: F \times F \to F$, a unary function symbol $-_F: F \to F$, a constant symbol $0_V$ of sort $V$, a function symbol $+_V: V \times V \to V$, a unary function symbol $-_V: V \to V$, and a function symbol $\mu: F \times V \to V$ for scalar multiplication.<br>

&emsp; An $L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-structure $M$ consists of two underlying sets $F^M$ and $V^M$, together with interpretations of the symbols of $L_{\mathrm{vs}}$:

$$\begin{aligned}0_F^M, 1_F^M \in F^M, \qquad +_F^M, \cdot_F^M: F^M \times F^M \to F^M, \qquad -_F^M: F^M \to F^M,\\
0_V^M \in V^M, \qquad +_V^M: V^M \times V^M \to V^M, \qquad -_V^M: V^M \to V^M,\end{aligned}$$

and

$$\mu^M: F^M \times V^M \to V^M.$$

&emsp; Let $T_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the $L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-theory consisting of the following axioms. First, the $F$-sort is a field under $0_F, 1_F, +_F, -_F, \cdot_F$. Second, the $V$-sort is an abelian group under $0_V, +_V, -_V$. That is, the following axioms hold.

1. $\forall u, v \in V \quad u +_V v = v +_V u.$
2. $\forall u, v, w \in V \quad (u +_V v) +_V w = u +_V (v +_V w).$
3. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V 0_V = v.$
4. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V (-_V v) = 0_V.$

Finally, the scalar multiplication symbol $\mu$ satisfies the following axioms:

5. $\forall v \in V \quad \mu(1_F, v) = v.$
6. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad \mu(a \cdot_F b, v) = \mu(a, \mu(b, v)),$
7. $\forall a \in F \ \forall u, v \in V \quad \mu(a, u +_V v) = \mu(a, u) +_V \mu(a, v),$
8. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad \mu(a +_F b, v) = \mu(a, v) +_V \mu(b, v),$

A model of $T_{\mathrm{vs}}$ is called a *vector space.*
