**Definition 1.1.1.** Let $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{field}}$ be the first-order language $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{field}} = \{0, 1, +, -, \cdot\}$, where $0$ and $1$ are constant symbols, $+$ and $\cdot$ are binary function symbols, and $-$ is a unary function symbol.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.1.2.** Let $T_{\mathrm{field}}$ be the $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{field}}$-theory which consists of the following axioms:

1. $\forall a,b,c \quad (a + b) + c = a + (b + c),$
2. $\forall a, b \quad a + b = b + a,$
3. $\forall a \quad a + 0 = a,$
4. $\forall a \quad a + (-a) = 0,$
5. $\forall a, b, c \quad (a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c),$
6. $\forall a, b \quad a \cdot b = b \cdot a,$
7. $\forall a \quad a \cdot 1 = a,$
8. $\forall a, b, c \quad a \cdot (b + c) = (a \cdot b) + (a \cdot c),$
9. $0 \neq 1,$
10. $\forall a \quad a \neq 0 \rightarrow \exists b \quad a \cdot b = 1.$

<br>

**Definition 1.1.3.** An $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{field}}$-structure ${\mathbb F}$ is called a *field* if ${\mathbb F}\vDash T_{\mathrm{field}}$, and $a$ is called a *scalar* if $a\in {\mathbb F}$.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.1.4.** Let $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the two-sorted first-order language with sorts $F$ and $V$. The language contains constant symbols $0_F, 1_F$ of sort $F$, function symbols $+_F, \cdot_F: F \times F \to F$, a unary function symbol $-_F: F \to F$, a constant symbol $0_V$ of sort $V$, a function symbol $+_V: V \times V \to V$, a unary function symbol $-_V: V \to V$, and a function symbol$\cdot_{FV}: F \times V \to V$ for scalar multiplication.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.1.5.** Let $T_{\mathrm{vs}}$ be the $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-theory consisting of the following axioms. First, the $F$-sort is a field under $0_F, 1_F, +_F, -_F, \cdot_F$. Second, the $V$-sort is an abelian group under $0_V, +_V, -_V$. That is, the following axioms hold:

1. $\forall u, v \in V \quad u +_V v = v +_V u,$
2. $\forall u, v, w \in V \quad (u +_V v) +_V w = u +_V (v +_V w),$
3. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V 0_V = v,$
4. $\forall v \in V \quad v +_V (-_V v) = 0_V.$

Finally, the scalar multiplication symbol $\cdot_{FV}$ satisfies the following axioms:

5. $\forall v \in V \quad 1_F \cdot_{FV} v = v,$
6. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad (a \cdot_F b)\cdot_{FV} v = a \cdot_{FV} (b \cdot_{FV} v),$
7. $\forall a \in F \ \forall u, v \in V \quad a \cdot_{FV} (u +_V v) = (a \cdot_{FV} u) +_V (a \cdot_{FV} v),$
8. $\forall a, b \in F \ \forall v \in V \quad (a +_F b) \cdot_{FV} v = (a \cdot_{FV} v) +_V (b \cdot_{FV} v).$
{:start="5"}

<br>

**Definition 1.1.6.** An $\mathcal L_{\mathrm{vs}}$-structure $\mathbb V$ is called a *vector space* if $\mathbb V \vDash T_{\mathrm{vs}}$, and $u$ is called a *vector* if $u\in \mathbb V$.<br>
<br>
