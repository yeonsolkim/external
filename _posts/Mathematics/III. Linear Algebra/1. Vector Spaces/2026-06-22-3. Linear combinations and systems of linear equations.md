**Definition 1.3.1.** Let $\mathbb V$ be a vector space and $S$ a nonempty subset of $\mathbb V.$ A vector $v\in \mathbb V$ is called a *linear combination* of vectors of $S$ if there exist a finite number of vectors $u_1, u_2,\dots, u_n\in S$ and scalars $a_1, a_2,\dots, a_n\in \mathbb F$ such that 

$$ v = a_1u_1 + a_2u_2  + \cdots + a_nu_n. $$

In this case, we also say that $v$ is a *linear combination of* $u_1, u_2,\dots, u_n\in S$ and call $a_1, a_2,\dots, a_n\in \mathbb F$ the *coefficients* of the linear combination.<br>
