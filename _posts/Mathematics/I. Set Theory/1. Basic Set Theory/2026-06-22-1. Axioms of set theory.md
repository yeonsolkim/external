**Definition 1.1.1.** Let $\mathcal L_{\in}$ be the first order language $\mathcal L_{\in}=\lbrace \in \rbrace,$ where $\in$ is a relation symbol.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.1.2.** Let $\text{ZFC}$ be the $\mathcal L_{\in}$-theory which consists of the following axioms:
1. Axiom of Extensionality.  $\forall A \forall B \ [\forall x(x \in A \leftrightarrow x \in B) \rightarrow A = B].$
2. Axiom of Empty Set.  $\exists E \forall x\ (x \notin E).$
3. Axiom of Pairing.  $\forall a \forall b \exists P \forall x \ [x \in P \leftrightarrow (x = a \vee x = b)].$
4. Axiom of Union.  $\forall A \exists U \forall x \ [x \in U \leftrightarrow \exists y(x \in y \wedge y \in A)].$
5. Axiom of Power Set.  $\forall A \exists P \forall x \ [x \in P \leftrightarrow \forall y\ (y \in x \rightarrow y \in A)].$
6. Axiom of Infinity.  $\exists I \ [\exists e\ (e \in I \wedge \forall z\ (z \notin e)) \wedge \forall x\ (x \in I \rightarrow \exists y\ (y \in I \wedge \forall z\ (z \in y \leftrightarrow (z \in x \vee z = x))))].$
7. Axiom Schema of Separation.  For every formula $\varphi(x, \vec{p})$ in $\mathcal{L}_{\in}$, where $B$ is not free in $\varphi,$ $\forall A \forall \vec{p} \exists B \forall x \ [x \in B \leftrightarrow (x \in A \wedge \varphi(x, \vec{p}))].$
8. Axiom Schema of Replacement.  For every formula $\varphi(x, y, \vec{p})$ in $\mathcal{L}_{\in}$, where $B$ is not free in $\varphi,$ $\forall A \forall \vec{p}\ [\forall x\ (x \in A \rightarrow \exists! y \varphi(x, y, \vec{p})) \rightarrow \exists B \forall y\ (y \in B \leftrightarrow \exists x\ (x \in A \wedge \varphi(x, y, \vec{p})))].$
9. Axiom of Foundation.  $\forall A \ [\exists x\ (x \in A) \rightarrow \exists x\ (x \in A \wedge \neg \exists y\ (y \in x \wedge y \in A))].$
10. Axiom of Choice.  $\forall A \ [\forall x\ (x \in A \rightarrow \exists y\ (y \in x)) \rightarrow \exists C \forall x\ (x \in A \rightarrow \exists! y\ (y \in x \wedge y \in C))].$
