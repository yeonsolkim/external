**Definition 6.1.1.** Let $X_1,\ldots,X_n\in \mathbb R$ and let $D=X_1\times\cdots\times X_n.$ A *function of* $n$ *variables* with domain $D$ is a function

$$f:D\longrightarrow \mathbb R.$$

<br>

**Definition 6.1.2.** Let $f:D\to \mathbb R$ be a function of two variables and let $k\in f(D).$ A *level curve* of $f$ is the curve with the equation $f(\mathbf x) = k.$ A collection of level curves is called a *contour map.*<br>
<br>

**Definition 6.1.3.** Let $f:D\to \mathbb R$ be a function of three variables and let $k\in f(D).$ A *level surface* of $f$ is the surface with the equation $f(\mathbf x) = k.$<br>
<br>

**Definition 6.1.4.** Let $f:D\to \mathbb R$ be a function of $n$ variables. We define a limit of $f$ as follows: 

$$\lim_{\mathbf  x\to\mathbf  a}f(\mathbf  x)=L\Longleftrightarrow \forall \varepsilon >0\ \exists \delta >0\ \forall \mathbf x\in D\ \left( 0<\|\mathbf  x-\mathbf a\| <\ \delta \Rightarrow | f(\mathbf x)-L| <\varepsilon\right).$$

The negation can be written as follows: 

$$\forall L\in \mathbb R\ \forall \varepsilon >0\ \exists \delta >0\ \exists\mathbf  x\in D\ \left( 0<\|\mathbf  x-\mathbf a\| <\ \delta \land \varepsilon \le | f(\mathbf x)-L| \right).$$

<br>

$$ \lim_{(x,y)\to (0,0)} \frac{x^2-y^2}{x^2+y^2} = L.$$
