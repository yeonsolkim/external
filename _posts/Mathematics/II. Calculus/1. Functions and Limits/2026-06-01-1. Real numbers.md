**Definition 1.1.1.** Let $x\in \mathbb R.$ 
1. $0$ is the number such that $x+0=x$ for all $x.$
2. $1$ is the nonzero number such that $x\cdot 1=x$ for all $x.$
3. We define the *negative* $x,$ denoted by $-x,$ as the number such that $x+(-x)=0.$
4. Let $x\ne0.$ We define the *reciprocal* of $x,$ denoted by $1/x,$ as the number such that $x\cdot (1/x)=1.$

<br>

**Definition 1.1.2.** Let $x\in \mathbb R.$ We define the *absolute value* of $x$ as follows:

$$
\vert x\vert  :=
\begin{cases}
x, & x \ge 0,\\
-x, & x < 0.
\end{cases}
$$

<br> 

**Theorem 1.1.3.** Let $x,y\in \mathbb R.$ Then

1. $\vert x\vert  \ge 0$
2. $\vert x\cdot y\vert  = \vert x\vert \cdot \vert y\vert$
3. $\vert x+y\vert  \le \vert x\vert +\vert y\vert$
4. $\vert x\vert ^2 = x^2$
5. $\vert x\vert  = 0 \Rightarrow x = 0$

<br>

**Definition 1.1.4** (Square Root). Let $x,y\in \mathbb R$ and $x\ge 0.$

$$
\sqrt{x}=y \Longleftrightarrow y \ge 0,\ y^2=x
$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.1.5.** Let $x\in \mathbb R.$ Then 

1. $\sqrt{x^2}=\vert x\vert$
2. $(\sqrt{x})^2=x$

<br>

**Definition 1.1.6.**

1. (Harmonic Mean). For $x+y\ne 0,$

	$$
	HM(x,y):=\frac{2xy}{x+y}
	$$

2. (Geometric Mean). For $xy\ge 0,$

	$$
	GM(x,y):=\sqrt{xy}
	$$

3. (Arithmetic Mean).

	$$
	AM(x,y):=\frac{x+y}{2}
	$$

4. (Quadratic Mean).

	$$
	QM(x,y):=\sqrt{\frac{x^2+y^2}{2}}
	$$

5. (Weighted Arithmetic Mean). For $w_i\ge 0$ and $\sum_{i=1}^{n}w_i=1,$

	$$
	WAM(x_1,\ldots,x_n):=\sum_{i=1}^{n}w_i x_i
	$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.1.7.**

$$
\min(x_1,\ldots,x_n) \le \operatorname{mean}(x_1,\ldots,x_n) \le \max(x_1,\ldots,x_n)
$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.1.8.**

$$
HM \le GM \le AM \le QM
$$
