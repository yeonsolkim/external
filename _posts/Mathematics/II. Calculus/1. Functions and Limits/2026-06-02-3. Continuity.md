**Definition 3.1** A function $f$ is $continuous$ at $x=a$ if 

$$ \lim_{x\to a}f(x)=f(a).$$

We say $f$ is $discontinuous$ at $x=a$ if $f$ is not continuous at $x=a$.<br>
<br>

**Example 3.1** Where is the following function discontinuous?

$$ f(x) = \left \lfloor x \right \rfloor. $$

**Solution.** floor function은 $x$보다 크지 않은 최대의 integer를 출력한다. 경향을 보기 위해 function plot을 그려보자. (See Figure 3.1.) discontinuous가 의심되는 지점은 어떤 $n\in \mathbb Z$에 대해 $x=n$인데 $\lim_{x\to n^-}f(x)+1=\lim_{x\to n^+}f(x)$가 되어 limit이 존재하지 않음을 알 수 있다.

**Definition 3.2** A function $f$ is $continuous$ $from$ $the$ $right$ at $x=a$ if 

$$ \lim_{x\to a^+}f(x)=f(a),$$

and $f$ is $continuous$ $from$ $the$ $left$ at $x=a$ if 

$$ \lim_{x\to a^-}f(x)=f(a).$$

<br>

**Definition 3.3** A function $f$ is $continuous$ $on$ $an$ $interval$ if it is continuous at every $x$ in the interval. (If $f$ is defined only on one side of an endpoint of the interval, we understand continuous at the endpoint to mean continuous from the right or continuous from the left.)<br>
<br>

**Example 3.2** Prove that the function $f(x)=1-\sqrt{1-x^2}$ is continuous on the interval $[-1,1]$.<br>
**Proof.**<br>
Lemma 1. Let $x\in [-1,1]$ and $a\in[0,1)$. Then, we have

$$ \frac{\vert x+a\vert}{\sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{1-a^2}}\leq \sqrt{\frac{1+a}{1-a}}.$$

The equality holds if and only if $x=1$.<br>
<br>

Lemma 2. Let $x\in [-1,1]$ and $a\in(-1,0)$. Then, we have

$$ \frac{\vert x+a\vert}{\sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{1-a^2}}\leq \sqrt{\frac{1-a}{1+a}}.$$

The equality holds if and only if $x=-1$.<br>
<br>

Claim 1. For all $x\in (-1,1)$, $f$ is continuous.<br>
Proof. We shall show that for all $a\in (-1,1)$, $\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=1-\sqrt{1-a^2}$ by epsilon-delta argument. Firstly, let $a\in[0,1)$ be given, and we choose 

$$\delta=\varepsilon\sqrt{\frac{1-a}{1+a}}.$$

Suppose that $\vert x-a\vert < \delta$. Then, by Lemma 1, we have: 

$$\vert \sqrt{1-x^2} - \sqrt{1-a^2}\vert = \vert x-a\vert \cdot \frac{\vert x+a\vert}{\sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{1-a^2}}<\delta \sqrt{\frac{1+a}{1-a}}=\varepsilon.$$

Secondly, let $a\in(-1,1)$ be given, and we choose 

$$\delta=\varepsilon\sqrt{\frac{1+a}{1-a}}.$$

Suppose that $\vert x-a\vert < \delta$. Then, by Lemma 2, we have: 

$$\vert \sqrt{1-x^2} - \sqrt{1-a^2}\vert = \vert x-a\vert \cdot \frac{\vert x+a\vert}{\sqrt{1-x^2} + \sqrt{1-a^2}}<\delta \sqrt{\frac{1-a}{1+a}}=\varepsilon.$$

Therefore, $\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=1-\sqrt{1-a^2}$. And, because it is trivial that $f(a)=1-\sqrt{1-a^2}$, the following holds: 

$$ \lim_{x\to a}f(x)=1-\sqrt{1-a^2}=f(a). $$

Claim 2. $f$ is continuous from the right at $x=-1$.<br>
<br>

Claim 3. $f$ is continuous from the left at $x=1$.<br>
<br>

By Claim 1, Claim 2, and Claim 3, $f(x)=1-\sqrt{1-x^2}$ is continuous on the interval $[-1,1]$. Q.E.D.
