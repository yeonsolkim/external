**Definition 1.1.** Suppose $y$ is a quantity that depends on another quantity $x$. The difference quotient 

$$ \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{f(x_2)-f(x_1)}{x_2-x_1} $$

is called the *average rate of change* of $y$ with respect to $x$ over the interval $[x_1,x_2]$. Alternatively, we can also consider the average rate of change based on a single point $(a,f(a))$ as follows: 

$$ \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}. $$

From this, we can define the *instantaneous rate of change* of $y$ with respect to $x$ at $(a,f(a))$ as follows: 

$$ \left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right\vert_{x=a}=\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}.$$ 

<br>

**Definition 1.2.** Let $f$ be a real-valued function. If 

$$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}=L$$

holds, we define the *derivative* of $f$ at $x=a$ to be $L$, denoted by $f'(a)$. In this case, $f$ is said to be *differentiable* at $x=a$, which means when the input change is sufficiently small, the rate of change is a constant More generally, if $I$ is an open interval and $f$ is differentiable at every point of $I$, then $f$ is said to be differentiable on $I$.<br>
<br>

**Remark 1.3.** Let $f$ be a function. When we draw the graph of $f$ on the Cartesian coordinate plane, the slope of the tangent line to $f$ at $x=a$ is equal to $f'(a)$.<br>
<br>

**Example 1.4.** Let $f:\mathbb R \to \mathbb R$ be the function such that $f(x)=\vert x \vert$. Where is $f$ differentiable?<br>
**Solution.** 어떤 $a$에서 "$L$ is a real number such that

$$\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{\vert a+h\vert -\vert a\vert}{h}=L$$

가 참"인지를 묻는 것이다. definition of absolute value로부터 조건별로 정의되어 있음을 알 수 있다. 따라서 경우를 나누는 수밖에 없겠다. 다만, 모든 경우를 볼 필요는 없다. $h\ne 0$가 충분히 $0$에 가깝다고 가정하고 $\vert a+h \vert$를 보자. $a>0$일 때에는 $a+h>0$임을 알 수 있다. 그러면 대칭적으로 $a<0\Rightarrow a+h< 0$일 것이다. 그리고 $a=0$이라면 $h$의 부호에 따라 결정된다. 따라서 전체 값은 이렇게 정리된다: 

$$  \frac{\vert a+h\vert -\vert a\vert}{h}= \begin{cases}
1 & \text{if }a > 0, \text{ or } a=0 \text{ and } h>0,\\
-1 & \text{if }a < 0, \text{ or } a=0 \text{ and } h<0.
\end{cases} $$

Definition 1.2에 의하여 $a\ne 0$일 때만이 the case이다.<br>
<br>

**Theorem 1.5.** If $f$ is differentiable at $x=a$, then $f$ is continuous at $x=a$.<br>
**Proof.** average rate of change가 constant일 때 input change가 $0$에 가까워지면 output change도 $0$에 가까워지는 것은 

$$

\text{output change}= \text{average rate of change}\times \text{input change}

$$

에 의해 자명하다. 그런데 이는 limit에서도 성립한다:

$$ \begin{aligned}
\lim_{x\to a}{[f(x)-f(a)]}
&= \lim_{x \to a}{\left[\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}\times(x-a)\right]}\\
&= \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a} \times \lim_{x\to a}{[x-a]}\\
&= L \times 0\\
&=0.
\end{aligned}$$

그리고

$$ \begin{aligned}
\lim_{x\to a}f(x)
&=\lim_{x\to a}{[f(a)+(f(x)-f(a))]}\\
&=\lim_{x\to a}f(a)+\lim_{x\to a}{[f(x)-f(a)]}\\
&=f(a).
\end{aligned}
$$

Q.E.D.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.6.** A function $f':D\to \mathbb R$ is the *derivative* of $f$ if there exists a function $f$ such that 

$$ f'(x)=\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$ 

for every input $x\in D$ where $D=\lbrace x\in \mathbb R : f'(x) \text{ exists}\rbrace$. <br>
<br>

**Example 1.7.** Let $f:\mathbb R \to \mathbb R$ be a function such that $f(x)=x^3-x$. Find the derivative of $f$.<br>
**Solution.** By the Definition 1.6, 

$$f'(x)=\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}=\lim_{h \to 0}{(3x^2+3hx+h^2-1)}=3x^2-1.$$

Because the limit exists for all $x\in \mathbb R$, $\operatorname{dom}(f')=\mathbb R$.<br>
