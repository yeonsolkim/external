**Definition 2.1.** Let $I$ be an open interval in $\mathbb R$. The *differentiation operator* $D$ is the operator that sends each differentiable function $f : I \to \mathbb R$ to its derivative $f'$. In other words, $Df$ is the function defined by

$$
(Df)(x) = f'(x)
$$

for every $x$ in $I$.<br>
<br>

**Theorem 2.2.** Let be $\mathbf c$ a constant function and $\mathrm{id}$ the identity function. Then
1. $D\mathbf c=0$
2. $D(\mathrm{id})=1$

<br>

**Theorem 2.3.** If $f,g$ are differentiable function and $\mathbf c$ is a constant function, then  
1. $D(f+g)=Df+Dg$
2. $D(f-g)=Df-Dg$
3. $D(\mathbf c f)=\mathbf c Df$ 
4. $D(fg)=(Df)g+f(Dg)$
