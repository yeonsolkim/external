**Theorem 3.2.1 (The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part 1).**  Let $a,b\in \mathbb R$ and $a\leq b.$ If a function $f$ is continuous on $[a,b],$ then the function $g:[a,b]\to \mathbb R$ defined by 

$$ g(x) = \int_a^x f(t)\,dt $$

is continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on $(a,b),$ and $g'(x)=f(x)$ for $x\in [a,b].$ <br>
*Proof.*
