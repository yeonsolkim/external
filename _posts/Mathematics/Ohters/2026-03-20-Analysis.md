**Definition 1.1.** Let $S$ be a set. An *order* on $S$ is a relation, denoted by $<$ with the following two properties.<br>
(1) For all $x,y\in S$, one and only one of the statements
 
$$x<y,\ x=y,\ y<x$$

is true.<br>
(2) $\forall x,y,z\in S (x<y\wedge y<z \Rightarrow x<z)$ <br>
<br>

**Definition 1.2.** Suppose $S$ is an ordered set, and $E\subset S$. If there exists a $\beta \in S$ such that $\forall x\in E, x\leq \beta$, we say that $E$ is *bounded above*, and call $\beta$ an *upper bound* of $E$.<br>
<br>

**Definition 1.3.** Suppose $S$ is an ordered set, and $E\subset S$, and $E$ is bounded above. Suppose there exists an $\alpha\in S$ with the following properties:<br>
(1) $\alpha$ is an upper bound of $E$.<br>
(2) If $\gamma <\alpha$ then $\gamma$ is not an upper bound of $E$.<br>
Then $\alpha$ is called the *supremum* of $E$, an we write 

$$\alpha =\sup E.$$

Also, the statement 

$$\alpha =\inf E$$

means that $\alpha$ is a *infimum* of $E$. <br>
<br>

**Definition 1.4 .** An ordered set $S$ is said to have the *least-upper-bound property(LUB)* if the following is true:

$$\forall E\subset S(E\neq \emptyset\wedge E \text{ is bounded above} \Rightarrow \exists u \in S(u=\sup E))$$

<br>

**Theorem 1.5 .** *Suppose $S$ is an ordered set with the LUB, $B\subset S, B\neq \emptyset, B$ is bounded below. Let $L$ be the set of all lower bounds of $B$. Then,*

$$\exists u\in S(u=\sup L),\ \inf B=\sup L.$$ 

<br>

**Definition 1.6.** A *field* is a set $F$ with two operations, which satisfy the followings: <br>
(A) Addition<br>
(A1) $\forall x,y\in F, x+y\in F$<br>
(A2) $\forall x,y\in F, \,x+y=y+x$<br>
(A3) $\forall x,y,z\in F, \,(x+y)+z=x+(y+z)$<br>
(A4) $\exists y\in F\,(\forall x\in F,\,x+y=x)$ and we write $y=0$<br>
(A5) $\forall x\in F, \exists y\in F\,(x+y=0)$ and we write $y=-x$<br>
<br>

(M) Multiplication<br>
(M1) $\forall x,y\in F,\,x\cdot y\in F$<br>
(M2) $\forall x,y\in F,\,x\cdot y=y\cdot x$<br>
(M3) $\forall x,y,z\in F,\,(x\cdot y)\cdot z=x\cdot (y\cdot z)$<br>
(M4) $\exists y\in F\,((\forall x\in F,\,x\cdot y=x)\wedge(y\neq 0))$ and we write $y=1$<br>
(M5) $\forall x\in F\,(x\neq 0),\,\exists y\in F\,(x\cdot y=1)$ and we write $y=1/x$<br>
<br>

(D) Distribution

$$\forall x,y,z\in F,\,x\cdot (y+z)=x\cdot y+y\cdot z$$
