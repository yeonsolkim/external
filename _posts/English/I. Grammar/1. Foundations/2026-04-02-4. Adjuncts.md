## 1. Modifier
#### 1.1. The modifier to an NP
restrictive: modifier가 집합의 범위를 좁힘.
> Examples.<br>
> students who came late<br>
> smart students 

#### 1.2. PP functioning as the supplement to a clause
**He was awarded the prize because he achieved outstanding accomplishments.**
* subject: he
* predicate: was awarded the prize
* modifier(PP): because he achieved outstanding accomplishments
  * head(P): because
  * complement(finite clause): he achieved outstanding accomplishments


## 2. Supplement

#### 2.1. Past-participial clauses functioning as the supplement to an NP
non-restrictive. <br>
**The Strange Situation Experiment, conducted by psychologist Mary Ainsworth in 1970s, determined the attachment behavior of infants toward their caregivers.**
* subject(NP)
  * head: the strange situation experiment
  * supplement(past-participial clause): conducted by psychologist mary ainsworth in 1970s
    * predicate: conducted by psychologist mary ainsworth in 1970s
      * head: conducted
      * complement(PP): by psychologist mary ainsworth
      * modifier(PP): in 1970s
* predicate
  * head: determined
  * complement: the attachment behavior of infants toward their caregivers
    * determiner: the
    * modifier(NP): attachment
    * head: behavior
    * complement(PP): of infants
    * modifier(PP): toward their caregivers


#### 2.2. Finite clauses functioning as the supplement to an NP
non-restrictive. <br>
**The correct approach, it was realized only much later, is to consider the finite situation**
* subject(NP)
  * determiner(determinative): the
  * modifier(Adj): correct
  * head: approach
  * supplement(finite clause): it was realized only much later
