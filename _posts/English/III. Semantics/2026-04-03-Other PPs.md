### 1. By-PP
1. reason
2. unit
3. determining factor


> Examples.
> 1. It was marked <u>by a good or legitimate reason</u>.
> 2. They are paid <u>by the week</u>.
> 3. My scheme would stand or fall <u>by that principle</u>.


### 2. With-PP
1. the complement of an adjective (상태를 보충)
2. manner (동작 과정의 양태)
3. content

>Examples.
>1. I fell so ill <u>with hay fever</u>.
>2. He solved the problem <u>with great difficulty</u>: 그는 많은 우여곡절 끝에 문제를 풀었다.
>3. It worked out <u>with her help</u>.


### 3. As-PP
1. means
2. background (circumstantial/temporal)
3. content

>Examples.
>1. They ascertained exactly <u>as a result of research or calculation</u>.
>2. We join Wilson in 1911 <u>as he wonders whether he can re-create the scene in the laboratory</u>: circumstantial background
>3. The conference was acclaimed <u>as a considerable success</u>.

### 4. About-PP
1. domain

>Examples.
>1. There is much to learn <u>about current problems</u>. (much가 direct object이다.)


### 5. To-PP
1. purpose

>Examples.
>1. He uses his power <u>to bad effect</u>.
