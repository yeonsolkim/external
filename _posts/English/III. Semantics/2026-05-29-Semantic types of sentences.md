subject와 predicate의 의미를 기준으로 몇가지 type을 만들었다. 구체적 예시를 보고 가장 적합한 type을 만드는 top-down 방식으로 했기에, 각 type들은 대등하지 않을 수 있다: 어떤 것은 다른 것의 하위 type 또는 상위 type일 수 있다. 

### 1. Agent, Actioning

> Examples.
> 1. The company exported rice to Japan.
> 2. He ate the apple.
> 3. The teacher recommended that she be sent home.


### 2. Experiencer, Experiencing
experiencer(의지적 작용이 아니라 심리적, 감각적 인지를 경험하는 주체)

>Examples.
>1. I feel cold.
>2. She loves music.
>3. I believe she will come home.


### 3. Instrument, Functioning
>Examples.
>1. This key opens the back door.
>2. The road leads to the city center.


### 4. Theme, State
>Examples.
>1. I was hospitalized.
>2. English is spoken in many countries.
>3. The data is being processed.


### 5. Rule, Enforcing
>Examples.<br>
>The law forbids smoking in this area.


### 6. Antagonist, Actioning(작용)
>Examples. 
>1. The cold froze the river. 
>2. The music makes me happy.
>3. The simple interface disguises the system’s complexity.


### 7. Evidence, Indicating
>Examples.<br>
>The study suggests that caffeine improves memory.


### 8. Enabler, Enabling
enabler(event가 성립할 수 있는 상황적 조건을 마련하는 요소)
>Examples.
>1. This technology allows users to share files instantly.
>2. The road gives access to the village.


### 9. Source, Forming
>Examples.
>1. The environment I grew up in made me who I am today. 
>2. The river formed these deposits. 


### 10. Event, Driving
>Examples.
>1. That incident drove me to this state. (그 사건은 나를 이 지경으로 몰고갔어.)
>2. The mountain walk took us from Kreuth to Lake Achen.


### 11. Agonist, Undergoing
>Examples.
>1. These deposits were formed by the river.
