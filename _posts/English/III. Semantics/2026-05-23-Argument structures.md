main argument structures
1. (subject) intransitive verb
2. (subject) intransitive verb (predicative complement) 
3. (subject) transitive verb (complement)
4. (subject) transitive verb (direct object) (complement)
5. (subject) transitive verb (clause complement)

>Examples.
>1. (Physics) has been treading.
>2. (Discrepancies (that might appear at the cosmological level)) are ((extremely) difficult (to probe experimentally)).
>3. (Lord Kevin) talked (about two clouds).
>4. (He) congratulated (her) (on her promotion).<br>
>   (He) persuaded (her) (to change the tactics).<br>
>   (He) expected (her to change the tactics).
